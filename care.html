Candle Care

To ensure you are giving your candle the best life you possibly can,
follow these instructions before use:



1. Let the candle create a wax layer

In order to give the candle future opportunites to burn evenly and economically, it is reccomended that users allow for a "wet" layer of wax to accumulate before putting out the flame. This should take roughly four hours after it is first lit.



2. Use a snuffer to remove the flame

To prevent the candle from producing any more wase after use, place a snuffer on top of the wick for about 5 seconds. Once the flame is out, allow it to cool.



3. Keep your candle clean and use a lid

Remove any dust or dirt present on the candle after usage. Once clean, use a lid, if provided, to seal the contents of the candle for greater preservation.



4. Remove excess wax before using again

Wax residue will build up with each subsequent, and it is important to manage to stop it from spreading or burning yourself.



5. Remove any excess of the wick

Once burnt, the wick of a candle can no longer be used, and it will soon fall into the pit of wax below it. It is important to trim the wick regularly to ensure this doesn't happen, keeping it at roughly 6mm.



Why should I care?:

(and more frequently asked questions)



Why should I take care of my candle?:

Caring for your candle will guarantee a much longer, richer burn and more use from your purchase!



When should I stop using my candle?:

Don't attempt to use all of the wax the candle has, as the wick requires a solid foundation to burn properly. Once your wax measures at only 5mm, it's time to put it away.



How should I dispose of my candle?:

Was it in a glass jar? Remove any of the contents inside and use it as a jar for, anything else! Any removed contents can be disposed of in the garbage, but you should ensure they aren't dry in order to prevent a possible fire hazard.



Are scented candles safe for everyone to breath?:

While every candle is different, any candle provided by an official retailer or service is unlikely to cause any health disturbances or allergic reactions, unless otherwise specified.



How long can I leave my candle alone?:

While it is generally not encouraged to leave any open flames going alone, candles should not be left out any longer than 4 hours and should be structurally ensured not to spill or fall over.



Can I leave my candle alone around children or pets?:

No.
